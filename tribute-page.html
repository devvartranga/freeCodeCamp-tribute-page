Dr. B.R Ambedkar

The Life and Legacy of Dr. B.R. Ambedkar

Here is a timeline of Dr. Ambedkar's Life:

  • 1891 - Dr. Bhimrao Ramji Ambedkar was born on April 14, 1891, in Mhow, Madhya Pradesh, India.
  • 1906 - Ambedkar enrolled in Elphinstone College in Mumbai, becoming one of the first Dalits to get admission in a college.
  • 1913 - Ambedkar was awarded a scholarship by the Baroda State to study abroad, which led him to study at Columbia University in New York.
  • 1916 - He completed his MA in Economics from Columbia University and later pursued a D.Sc. in Economics from the London School of Economics.
  • 1923 - Ambedkar began to work on social reform, establishing the Bahishkrit Hitakarini Sabha, which worked towards the welfare of the lower castes and marginalized communities.
  • 1930 - Led the Kalaram Temple Entry Movement to allow Dalits to enter Hindu temples in Maharashtra.
  • 1932 - Ambedkar led the Poona Pact negotiations with Mahatma Gandhi to secure separate electorates for the Dalits, later called the “Depressed Classes.”
  • 1935 - Ambedkar was appointed as the principal of the Government Law College in Mumbai, where he continued his work on social reforms.
  • 1947 - After India’s independence, Dr. Ambedkar was appointed as the Chairman of the Drafting Committee of the Indian Constitution.
  • 1949 - The Indian Constitution was finalized under Ambedkar’s leadership, and he became known as the Father of the Indian Constitution.
  • 1950 - Dr. Ambedkar converted to Buddhism on October 14, along with thousands of his followers, in a movement against the caste system.
  • 1951 - Ambedkar published his “The Problem of the Rupee: Its Origin and Its Solution”, influencing India’s monetary policy.
  • 1954 - He wrote the book “The Untouchables: Who Were They?”, analyzing the social conditions of the untouchables.
  • 1956 - Dr. Ambedkar passed away on December 6, 1956, leaving behind a profound legacy of social justice and equality.
  • Posthumous Recognition - Dr. Ambedkar’s influence continues to be felt, and his legacy is celebrated through various memorials, his statue at the Ambedkar Memorial in New Delhi, and his writings on social justice and human rights.
